## The evidence, restated

- **Runtime gate verdict: NO-GO.** Advisor = **6.66 (Gemini prod) / 7.30 (Claude V6, best measured)**, both below the **≥7.5** bar. 6 of 7 gate requirements pass; the advisor-quality bar is the sole blocker (`LIOS_RUNTIME_GATE_REVIEW.md`).
- **Residual-gap decomposition (prod 6.66 → raw-Claude ceiling 8.00):** **~47% model capability + ~49% validator number gate + ~3% non-number rule suppression + ~1% prompt + 0% composition/compliance + 0% ARCHITECTURE** (`ROOT_CAUSE_ATTRIBUTION.md`). Architecture (the absence of LIOS) is a measured **0%** of the gap.
- **The two — and only two — proven levers:** (a) model capability (Gemini→Claude in a byte-identical pipeline = **+0.64**), (b) the number-gate fallbacks (clear the 6 fallback turns → LN+Claude ≈ **8.08** on passed turns, ≈ raw Claude). `CLAUDE_CONTROL_EXPERIMENT.md` Q1–Q5.
- **Governing rule (unchanged from the matrix):** a component that does not address (a) model capability or (b) the number-gate/fallback mechanism cannot, on current evidence, move the benchmark — so it is at best UNPROVEN against it. `CLAUDE_CONTROL_EXPERIMENT.md` Q5: "Neither an orchestrator, agents, critics, nor multi-agent execution touches either lever. LIOS is not on the critical path to advisor quality."
- **Prod-existence reality:** Relationship Manager, Recommendation Engine, Compliance/validator, Memory/context, GraphRAG, and the Scenario engines **already exist in prod** and were **held constant** in the Claude control experiment — where the pipeline retained **91% of raw-Claude quality (100% on passed turns)**, proving they add **no quality drag and no measured lift**. The proposed NEW pieces — multi-agent Orchestrator-as-LIOS, Critic, Scenario Engine _as a new investment_, Tradeoff Engine, per-domain Domain Agents — are **speculative** and do not exist (no `critic.py`; domains are deterministic).

## What would make each component JUSTIFIED (the measured weakness that must appear first)

- **Orchestrator (routing):** a coverage measurement on **data-rich, multi-turn, loaded-context** personas showing multi-domain turns the single advisor _measurably misses_ and a router _recovers_. Until that delta exists, routing is 0%.
- **Memory:** a **multi-turn** benchmark where persisted facts, when loaded, raise context-usage/personalization above the single-turn baseline by a measured margin.
- **Critic:** a measured rate of conceptually-wrong/over-confident advice on **high-stakes** turns that a critic pass demonstrably reduces — and a showing that the safer output also _scores_ higher, not just safer.
- **GraphRAG:** the loaded-context head-to-head ("does LN's stored-context personalization beat Claude-with-the-same-context-pasted-in?") returning a positive, measured personalization lift.
- **Scenario Engine (new investment):** a number-gate that _admits_ tool-generated/verified-derivation numbers, then a measured actionability lift attributable to richer projections rather than to the gate fix itself.
- **Tradeoff Engine:** a residual tradeoff-discovery gap that **survives** the V3 prompt fix and the Claude swap — i.e. a deficit the single-call reasoning provably cannot reach.
- **Domain Agents:** a per-domain quality deficit that persists _after_ the Claude swap (Claude currently wins every domain) and that fan-out closes — at a cost the $4/day envelope tolerates.
- **Recommendation Engine (new agent):** a measured actionability gap that remains after the number-gate fix and that an LLM recommender beats the live deterministic engine on.
- **Relationship Manager:** already JUSTIFIED as infra; nothing further required to keep it.

## Conclusion

**No NEW LIOS component is justified today.** This reassessment reconfirms the prior matrix against the latest evidence: the runtime gate is **NO-GO** (advisor 6.66 prod / 7.30 Claude, both < 7.5), and **architecture is a measured 0%** of the residual gap. The components that already exist in prod (Relationship Manager, Recommendation Engine, Compliance, Memory, GraphRAG, Scenario engines) are plumbing — held constant in the control experiment, they add no quality drag and no measured lift. The genuinely new pieces (Orchestrator-as-LIOS, Critic, Scenario Engine as new investment, Tradeoff Engine, Domain Agents) are UNPROVEN or recommended-against; none maps to a measured weakness.

**The gap is model capability (~47%) + the validator number gate (~49%).** Spend only on those two levers: refine the number gate (admit document-derived/tool-generated/verified numbers, strip-not-reject) and route high-stakes turns to Claude (test Sonnet first). **Revisit LIOS only after the advisor clears 7.5 via those two levers** — and even then, build components one at a time, each only when a measured weakness earns it a JUSTIFIED rating.
