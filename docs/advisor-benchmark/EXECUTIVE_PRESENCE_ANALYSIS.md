# Executive Presence Analysis

**Would a CFP / CFO / executive trust the voice?**

|            | LifeNavigator |  Claude | ChatGPT |
| ---------- | ------------: | ------: | ------: |
| Avg (0–10) |           6.1 | **8.5** |     7.2 |

## Read

LifeNavigator places **third** (−2.4 vs Claude, −1.1 vs ChatGPT). Two things hold it back:

1. **The formulaic opener.** A large share of LN replies begin with a near-identical stem — _"You're weighing a significant decision…" / "You've shared a clear snapshot…"_ The P0.5 VOICE work reduced clichés but the opening template is still sticky, and across 50 reads it lands as scripted rather than present. (This exact issue was flagged as a partial P0.5 win in the upgrade sprint; the benchmark confirms it persists.)
2. **Frame-then-ask cadence.** Stopping at a question reads as _tentative_ / intake-like to an executive audience, where presence comes from saying something substantive and standing behind it.

- **Claude** sounds like a senior advisor: calm, organized, confident, structured headers, and a clear point of view — without losing warmth ("I'm sorry to hear about your layoff. Here's what to prioritize immediately:").
- **ChatGPT** is crisp and confident ("Pay off the credit cards aggressively.") which reads as decisive presence even when terse.

## Implication

Executive presence is **half voice, half stance.** LN can win the voice half cheaply (kill the stock opener — a prompt fix). The stance half requires it to commit to an analysis, not just a question. See `ADVISOR_V2_ROADMAP.md` (P0 voice + P0 actionability).
