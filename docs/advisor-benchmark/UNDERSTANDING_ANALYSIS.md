# Understanding Analysis

**Did it understand the actual problem?**

|            | LifeNavigator |  Claude | ChatGPT |
| ---------- | ------------: | ------: | ------: |
| Avg (0–10) |           7.4 | **9.0** |     7.8 |

## Read

This is LifeNavigator's **strongest non-trivial dimension** and the gap is smallest here (−1.6 vs Claude). All three reliably parse the situation. The difference is _depth_ of understanding:

- **Claude** restates the surface ask **and** identifies the real underlying decision ("this depends on what energizes you and your long-term goals" for car-01), then proceeds on that understanding.
- **LifeNavigator** demonstrates understanding mainly by **mirroring the facts back** ("You've shared a clear snapshot… $185k income, $95k cash, $210k retirement, considering a $620k home"). Accurate, but it stops at restatement — understanding is _displayed_, not _used_.
- **ChatGPT** understands but compresses; it jumps to the answer with less explicit reflection.

## Why LN doesn't win even here

LN's reflection is genuinely good (the P0 work shows). But a strong restatement reads as "you understood my inputs," whereas Claude's reads as "you understood my _situation_." The judges consistently rewarded the latter.

## Implication

Understanding is **not** LN's problem. It is the one place LN is close. The losses are downstream — what it does _after_ understanding (frame-and-stop vs analyze-and-advise).
