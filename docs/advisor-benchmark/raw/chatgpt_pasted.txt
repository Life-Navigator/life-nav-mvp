### fin-01
Based on the numbers alone, you're in a reasonable position to buy, but I'd want to verify the monthly payment before committing.

A $620k home with 20% down would require about $124k upfront, leaving you with very little liquidity after closing costs. A more balanced approach may be 10–15% down while preserving emergency reserves.

Key questions:

What would the total monthly payment be (mortgage, taxes, insurance, HOA)?
Would it stay below roughly 25–30% of gross income?
Do you still have 6+ months of expenses afterward?

You have strong retirement savings for age 34 and meaningful cash reserves. If the payment is manageable and you plan to stay in the home at least 7–10 years, buying is reasonable. If there's uncertainty about location or employment, renting another few years is also defensible.
