# LIOS Foundation — Executive Summary

Evidence-based, brutally honest. Grounded in this program's benchmarks (advisor 7.60 on Gemini Pro clears the
gate; model is the quality lever; **architecture = 0% of the advisor gap**; the trust spine caught frontier-model
fabrications), the codebase (LIOS already exists ~70% in fragments), and pilot economics.

## The thesis in one line

**Models are interchangeable labor; the durable, auditable, multi-domain life model is the moat. LIOS owns the
moat.** Stop competing on answer quality; compete on owning the life model and the distribution to fill it.

## The 10 questions

1. **What is the LifeNavigator moat?** The **per-user, provenance-grounded, multi-domain Life Graph**, the
   **document→typed-life-model ingestion pipeline**, and the **model-agnostic trust/governance spine**. These
   compound with use, carry high switching cost, and are model-independent. Not the prompt, not the model.
2. **What belongs inside LIOS?** Ontology, relationships, graph (3-store), memory + lifecycle, the decision
   framework, the scenario framework, provenance, domain ownership, and model governance (registry + capability
   routing + trust spine). The durable assets.
3. **What does NOT belong inside LIOS?** The models (workers), agent swarms, recursive orchestration, autonomous
   loops, model experimentation, the UI, and the prompt-of-the-month. Replaceable/peripheral.
4. **What to build before pilot?** Essentially **no new LIOS**. Ship the pilot on what's live (advisor on Gemini
   Pro + health-safety + routing). The only pre-pilot work: the go-live checklist, **maximize life-graph
   accumulation in onboarding**, and **surface provenance in the UI**. Architecture is 0% of advisor quality —
   don't delay the pilot for it.
5. **What waits until after pilot?** Unified provenance record, first-class memory lifecycle, the API-side
   ontology registry, domain-seam resolution, then the scenario engine + risk/opportunity propagation, then
   desktop/local.
6. **What assets become more valuable over time?** The per-user life graph (every turn + document adds evidenced
   edges), the ontology breadth, the provenance corpus, the document-type coverage, and the eval/benchmark
   harness. These are network/accumulation effects _per user_.
7. **What assets are model-independent?** **All of LIOS** — ontology, graph, memory, decision framework,
   provenance, trust spine, domain ownership, governance. That independence (proven: the spine works across
   Gemini/Claude and caught Claude's fabrications) is the point.
8. **What makes LifeNavigator impossible to replicate quickly?** A user's accumulated, document-evidenced,
   cross-domain, provenance-tracked life graph — earned over months and not re-enterable elsewhere — multiplied
   by the integrated-domain breadth, the ingestion pipeline, and the audit trail. The prompt is a weekend; the
   life model + provenance is years.
9. **Shortest path to a defensible platform?** (a) Ship the pilot now (advisor is good enough); (b) **maximize
   life-graph accumulation** (document ingestion + structured capture) — that's the switching cost; (c) make
   **provenance a visible, sellable feature** (auditable advice vs ChatGPT's black box); (d) keep the
   model-agnostic trust spine + governance as the differentiator. Build DATA + TRUST first, not agents.
10. **Shortest path to a $100M+ company?** Not better answers (commoditizing). It is: **become the
    system-of-record for complex lives** (own the life graph) and **distribute through advisors / family offices
    / employers (B2B2C)** — they need a scalable, auditable, multi-domain intelligence layer they cannot build.
    The data + provenance moat unlocks fiduciary/enterprise trust and recurring revenue. Own the durable life
    model + the distribution; rent the models.

## The disciplined next moves

- **Now:** pilot go-live (checklist) + accumulation + visible provenance.
- **Post-pilot:** unify provenance + memory lifecycle + ontology registry (the durable core).
- **Never (until evidence demands it):** LIOS "Runtime," agent swarms, autonomous loops, model-count theater.

**Bottom line:** LifeNavigator's defensibility is the life model and the trust spine, not the model or the
prompt. Ship the pilot, fill the graph, surface provenance, and govern models as swappable workers — that is the
shortest path to both a defensible platform and a $100M+ company.
